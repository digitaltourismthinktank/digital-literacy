Spreadsheets and CSVs

The file format AI actually reads

Step 2 of 6 · The idea

A CSV is just text. Commas make the columns.

2025-06-05,Harbour Museum,455,2275
↓ each comma starts a new column ↓
2025-06-05
Harbour Museum
455
2275

That's the whole format. A comma starts a new column, a new line starts a new row, and there is nothing else: no colours, no formulas, no merged cells. That bareness is exactly why AI tools like CSVs, and exactly why a stray comma or a currency symbol can throw the whole thing off.

▶New to this? Start here30-second grounding
A spreadsheet (an Excel or Google Sheets file) is a working document. It can hold formatting, formulas, colours and several tabs. A CSV is just the data underneath, saved as plain text. CSV stands for "comma-separated values". When you "export to CSV", you keep the rows and columns and throw away everything else. AI tools work best with that stripped-back version because there is nothing extra to misread.
Step 3 of 6 · Quick check

One quick prediction before we move on.

In the messy file, one revenue figure was written as £1,945, with a comma inside the number and no quote marks around it. What will most tools do with it?

It's B. The comma is the column separator, so a comma inside an un-quoted value reads as "new column here". The £1,945 splits into £1 and 945, every later value on that row shifts one place to the right, and the AI can no longer line revenue up with the right day. This single problem is one of the most common reasons a data file misbehaves.
Step 4 of 6 · Choose your approach

Tools that open, check and clean CSVs.

ToolCostBest for
ExcelPaidMost people already have it. Strong for editing, but watch its habit of "helpfully" reformatting dates and dropping leading zeros.
Google SheetsFreeFree, shareable, opens CSVs cleanly. Good default for quick checks and light cleaning.
NumbersFreeComes with Mac. Fine for viewing and small edits; export back to CSV when you're done.
OpenRefineFreeBuilt for messy data at scale. Steeper to learn, but powerful for de-duping and fixing inconsistent values across thousands of rows.
AirtableFree / PaidSpreadsheet-database hybrid. Useful when the data needs structure and rules, not just a one-off tidy.
CSV LintFreeChecks a CSV for structural problems before you use it. A quick way to catch broken rows early.

No single right answer here. Pick what fits the job and what you already have open. For most quick fixes, a spreadsheet you know plus an AI tool is enough.

Step 6 of 6 · Carry forward

What to take with you.

  • A CSV is plain text: commas separate columns, line breaks separate rows. Nothing else.
  • The usual things that break AI tools are mixed date formats, numbers with symbols or commas, and stray title or total rows.
  • When AI misreads your data, suspect the file first. Cleaning it is often a single, repeatable prompt.
